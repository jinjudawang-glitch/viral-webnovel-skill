# Platform Patterns

Use this reference for Fanqie/Qimao-style commercial webnovel instincts. These are built-in patterns, not real-time rankings. If the user asks for latest trends, browse current sources and cite them.

## Reader Desire

The target reader usually wants fast emotional reversal:

- The protagonist is underestimated, humiliated, robbed, expelled, mocked, or trapped.
- The first visible win arrives quickly, but the deeper situation gets worse.
- Enemies are not just defeated; they are made to realize they misread the protagonist.
- Every win opens a bigger debt, secret, enemy, map, or resource conflict.

Define the reader desire before drafting:

- What pressure should be reversed?
- What punishment will feel deserved?
- What protagonist behavior feels satisfying: cold, mouthy, shameless, ruthless, patient, or reckless?
- What unanswered expectation should carry the next 3-5 chapters?

## Title Hooks

Good commercial titles often contain one of these engines:

- Identity inversion: "废骨", "弃徒", "杂役", "罪子", "替死", "炉鼎", "废脉".
- Cheat abnormality: "吞", "献祭", "偷天", "反噬", "污染", "借命", "改命".
- Scale promise: "万界", "九天", "诸天", "神狱", "天命", "道祖", "帝尊".
- Immediate contradiction: weak label plus impossible power.

Avoid titles that only sound grand but reveal no conflict.

## Synopsis Pattern

Use 3 beats:

1. Pressure: who crushed the protagonist and what did they take?
2. Abnormality: what rule breaks around the protagonist?
3. Promise: what kind of repeated payoff will the book deliver?

Keep the synopsis concrete. Do not explain the whole world.

## Common Drop-Off Points

- Chapter 1 spends too long on backstory.
- The cheat is generic and has no cost.
- Antagonists only shout and misunderstand.
- The protagonist wins without choice or consequence.
- The same face-slapping scene repeats with new names.
- Cultivation realms become numbers without scene meaning.
- The story explains rules instead of showing them through conflict.

## Platform Boundary Notes

Keep the fiction dark, ruthless, strange, and commercial, but avoid:

- Explicit sexual content.
- Sexualized minors.
- Real political incitement.
- Real-world targeted abuse.
- Excessively graphic gore.
- Direct imitation of a named copyrighted work or living author's style.
